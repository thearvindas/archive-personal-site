rules, why I need them, and why I'm writing them down [keeps updating]

Nov 25 2021

A while ago, I stumbled across Aditya Mohanty’s blog and saw this — a collection of rules that he lives by. Being someone that loves defining every aspect of my life and committing to a certain standard of ethics, I decided to make a list of rules for myself as well.

To save your time, I’ve listed my rules first, the chit-chat comes later.

Rules

[last updated on Dec 16, 2021]

Mind & Body#

  1. Nothing is more important than family. Everything you’re doing is for Ma, Papa, Sister (Fun fact: I call my sister “Sister”, funny story (not really) but definitely for another time).
  2. Always give your work 100%. No slacking off on things you are committed to.
  3. Patience is power.
  4. Don’t complain — learn, pick up the positive takeaways, and move on.
  5. Give back when you get the opportunity to — always help wherever you can.
  6. Gratitude above all.
  7. Respect everyone and their choices.
  8. Never lose your calm. “As soon as a fighter loses his temper, he has lost.” (Read this here )
  9. You attract what you are. You are what you attract.
  10. Never indulge in anything that may lead to losing control of your mind and body, however temporary it may be.
  11. Trust the process.

Life#

  1. Eat good food and don’t skip meals. An unhealthy lifestyle isn’t cool.
  2. Always carry a pair of earphones when heading outside.
  3. Read books.
  4. Clean up and cut down your following on social media platforms.
    (I cleaned up my following list one fine day and cut it down to ONLY my friends. Everything else: out. Benefits? No more endless scroll. No more constant memes. No more time wasted on something that adds zero value. Just pictures and moments from friends I love. Big win.)
  5. Sleep early. Wake up early.
  6. Spend some time alone. You don’t always have to be a social animal.
  7. Speak to Ma everyday.
  8. Never cheat. Lose in humility rather than win in disgrace.
  9. Maintain good posture.
  10. Write things down more.
  11. Drink enough water.
  12. There’s never too many gratitude lists.

fin.

Now if you want to know why I need rules and why I’m writing these down, read on.

Simply put, I love cut-throat discipline. It keeps things as they should be. It defines what can be done. When I live my life with a certain guideline of rules in mind, I allow myself to always be on top of what I’m doing, my decisions, and my goals. I know that these are what my principles are and this is what I should/shouldn’t do.

I’m writing this down because that’s what commitment is about. Aditya says he reads his rules every few days to remind himself to practice what he preaches. It made sense to me, and hence I wrote my rules down too. If you find these rules to resonate with you, I’m instantly fond of you. If these rules don’t, I respect that — we’re all different and that’s what makes the world such a great place.

Peace out.
