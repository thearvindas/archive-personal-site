productivity, leaning forward, and pomodoro

Sep 26 2021

I’ve been going down for walks these days and have picked up a habit of listening to podcasts. Today I listened to this podcast by Rob Dial where he speaks about 6 habits to boost one’s productivity. Here’s what I learnt:

Everyone has 24 hours in a day. The difference is in how these 24 hours are spent. To be productive is to maximize outcomes in the time we have — getting more things done in the same amount of time.

  1. Get moving: action creates action. If you want to get stuff done, make that first move and get up. Get out of bed. Move around for a couple of minutes. Have a glass of water. Tidy up your workspace. Always be leaning forward — the mindset of taking action and moving towards the goals you’ve set. It’s about having a get sh%t done attitude. Save your excuses. When you replace “I don’t have time” with “I probably don’t care enough”, you’ll realize how much you lose out with excuses. It’s easy to say that you don’t have time, but difficult to fake that you don’t care.

  2. Learn while you’re on the go: being productive is about getting more things done in the same amount of time. This means you make the most out of the time spent in passive work like travelling, brushing your teeth, or cleaning up your room. See where you can add space to learn more. Perhaps listen to a podcast or an audiobook. Catch up with email when you’re on the go.

  3. Use the Pomodoro technique: this is one of the best ways to be productive. Do 25 minutes of hardcore work followed by 5 minutes of hardcore rest. Repeat. Top tip: if you’re sitting down for work, use these 5 minutes to walk around, stretch, and take a breather.

  4. Plan out your day: don’t imitate the schedule of someone you look up to. Find what works for you. What time do you get the most work done? What time do you feel sleepy? What time do you feel most energetic? Shape your schedule around based on this. The best schedule is the one that works for you.

  5. Have a cutoff time: one might think that productivity is about a non-stop hustle. In reality, to be very productive, it’s important to take time off. Keep driving your car forever, it’ll ultimately run out of fuel and stop. Just like you plan your work periods, also plan your breaks — time that you spend taking care of yourself. Make this a regular thing.

  6. Prepare the night before: get ready for tomorrow, today. Make the next day easier for you in whatever capacity you can. You’ll wake up the next day and thank yourself. Clean up your table, tidy up your room, keep your clothes ready, fill up your water bottle. Get some good rest. Go to sleep with gratitude, wake up and crush your goals.

I’ve heard about the Pomodoro technique a lot but have never been serious about implementing it. Let me give it a good try this week. I’ll update this blog with the results.

Peace out.

Update (October 3rd, 2021): interesting outcome, definitely a nice way of getting work done. Not sure it works with my personal style, though. I get more stuff done with longer stretches of work. Oh well, not everything is for everyone. Do what works best for you =)
