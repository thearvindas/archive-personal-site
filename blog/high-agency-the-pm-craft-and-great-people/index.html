high agency, the PM craft, and great people

Sep 11 2021

Recently watched this video of Shreyas Doshi talking about his PM learnings over the years, and immediately knew I had to document it right away.

Here are my notes from the video:

Advice for aspiring PMs:

  1. Do the role before you get the job
  2. Your current company/work is the best place to start
  3. Build your own side project/product

The Law of PM Suitability: the traits required to break into the PM role are the very same traits that are prerequisites for succeeding as a PM

High Agency: This is the most vital trait for a PM. It’s about finding a way to get what you want, without waiting for conditions to be perfect or otherwise, blaming the conditions.

There are 4 types of people:

  1. Low agency + low talent: cogs-in-the-wheel
  2. Low agency + high talent: frustrated geniuses
  3. High agency + low talent: go-getters
  4. High agency + high talent: game changers

High agency can be broken down into two parts:

  1. Traits: ownership mindset, self-confidence, resilience
  2. Skills: creative execution, influential communication

PS: read The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People to understand best practices to cultivate high agency, have briefly summed it up at the end of this blog

A PM is responsible for turning adversaries into advocates.

The 3 essential senses of a PM:

  1. product sense
  2. analytical sense
  3. execution sense

Strategy for early PM growth:

  1. eliminate liabilities
  2. bring below-median skills upto median
  3. turn strengths into superpower

Too many PMs assume that the best way to grow is to dedicate 110% of their time to ongoing projects at work. A better approach is to spend 80% of time on ongoing projects and 20% on focused study.

What to learn: As a PM, one must learn to work at the intersection of art (vision, creativity, emotional appeal, intuition, delight) and science (results, biz, prioritization, data, utility)

Advice about joining companies: Join product-first companies, which means:

  1. greatest biz leverage is gained from product
  2. all functions obsess over building the best product experience
  3. they have an expansive view of the product

Tl;dr, as mentioned by Shreyas:

  1. Cultivate high agency
  2. Love the PM craft
  3. Work with great people

Peace out.

Bonus: A very very brief summary of The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People#

Change comes from the inside out.

Habit 1: Be proactive

  1. “I will solve this problem.”
  2. “I will take responsibility.”

Habit 2: Begin with the end in mind

  1. “This is what I want to achieve.”
  2. “I need to do things that align with my goals.”

Habit 3: Put first things first

  1. “My goals are above everything else.”
  2. “I can’t do this because it doesn’t help me reach the end result I want.”

Habit 4: Think win-win

  1. “I’m so happy for you.”
  2. “I want to help people out because life isn’t a zero-sum game.”

Habit 5: Seek first to understand, then to be understood

  1. “I need to understand this before I can work on it.”
  2. “I need to listen with intent to understand, not reply.”

Habit 6: Synergize

  1. “I need to be a team player and help people around me, add value wherever I can.”
  2. “I need to look at things with an open mind.”

  1. “I need to improve my life every single day â€” exercising, taking enough rest, reading, writing, listening to music, organizing, volunteering, and helping people out.”
  2. “The biggest win is self-improvement and to be better than who I was yesterday.”
