three P0s, four quarters, and five-times-two things I never regret

Dec 29 2021

Very recently, I came across this tweet by Sahil Bloom.

I was taken aback by the simplicity of the idea yet how much sense it made to me right away. Heading into the new year, figured that this was a framework I could try out.

What are a few things I never regret?

  1. I never regret waking up early.
  2. I never regret working out
  3. I never regret going out for a walk with a pair of earphones
  4. I never regret time spent with family and my best friends
  5. I never regret cutting down on screen times
  6. I never regret eating healthy
  7. I never regret spending time with my guitar
  8. I never regret time spent writing
  9. I never regret time spent learning and reading
  10. I never regret maintaining good posture

Just writing this down gives me a sense of why I should do these more. Sahil says do more of these, spend more time on these things. I think that gives me an idea of my approach in 2022. Because you see, or you might know if you’ve read my previous posts, I like planning out each aspect of my life. Having a framework of what I’m looking to do entering 2022 helps me feel confident about the year.

I think new year’s resolutions is something thrown a lot but it takes a lot of effort to commit. Perhaps why habit building is so important. This is something else I’m looking to do. By now I have a decent idea of where I’m headed in life, so it’s time I start shaping good habits and trying to improve every single day.

What are some habits I’m looking to pick up?

  1. A consistent sleep schedule (preferably waking up by 4:30-5)
  2. Better commitment towards working out and exercise
  3. Some time put aside everyday for writing/introspection
  4. Stretching a lot more, posture exercises
  5. Mapping out and planning each day better for optimum use of time

That reminds me, something interesting I’ve started doing to manage my time better is dividing my day into 4 quarters. Found this post on Reddit and it blew my mind.

Started doing it immediately. A rough breakdown of how I’m mapping out my quarters and what goals I generally have attached to each:

  • q1 [0 - 6]: good sleep + wake up by 4:30-5
  • q2 [6 - 12]: breakfast + plan out the day + finish off one P0 task
  • q3 [12 - 18]: lunch + finish off another P0 task + exercise
  • q4 [18 - 24]: dinner + finish off the final P0 task for the day + sleep by 22:30-23

Restricting myself to just 3 P0 tasks each day has helped me achieve more, also gives me more time to finish off smaller P1 and P2 tasks when I have the time.

Alright, I think that’s it for this one. This blog post was just a lot of mapping out what I’m doing and want to do. Apologies if it was boring. You can think of this as me committing publicly. (Feel free to give me a hard time if you see me not following any of these things.)

Peace out.
