hunting, shipwrecks, and a washed out Grand Prix

Aug 30 2021

Before I begin, here are a few things I’d like to clear up:

  1. I’ve been noticing that the titles of these blog posts have been increasing in length each time. Although I would like them all to be the same length simply for good visuals, I can’t find a way to keep them concise. Hence, if you’re looking at the list of posts and noticing the gradual increase, don’t worry, it’s been bothering me too.
  2. The landing page of this website says “This is a documentation of my learnings.”, and that’s probably what one expects when they open any of my posts. This blog, however, isn’t about any of my learnings, if that wasn’t evident so far.

The internship hunt is still ongoing, I haven’t been able to convert any cold emails so far. Not entirely sure what’s not working out, so I spend a lot of time iterating on what I can do better with the next email. I can’t wait to land my first role and learn loads, but this hunt itself has been a learning experience each step of the way.

I’ve been building a habit of getting back to reading and although I haven’t fully kicked off, I understand why reading is such a brilliant practice. Something that is absolutely stuck in my head is a line I read in Indistractable , a quote by Paul Virilio: “When you invent the ship, you also invent the shipwreck”. What a brilliant line. It speaks about the negatives that are created in parallel with technological progress—maybe not by design, but because of the natural balance that the system creates.

If you’ve read the first blog here, you would know that I’m a huge fan of F1. So, let’s take a moment to speak about this week’s Grand Prix. Spa is my favourite circuit, no cap. Alright, maybe excluding street circuits because I’ve got a bit of a soft spot for Baku. But, yes, Spa is a race I look forward to SO much. What a shame for it to get washed out because of all the rain. Let’s remember, we do race in the rain, the conditions in Spa were just too horrible. I usually like the rain, but when it ruins my chances of watching cutthroat overtakes on Eau Rouge and flat outs down Pouhon, I gain a particular disliking for it. Not going to be too disappointed though, what a great moment it was watching George pick up his first podium after outclassing the pack in qualifying in a Williams. A true tale of hard work paying off and always giving 100% no matter what. Maybe I’m even happier for the folks at Williams. The crew works tirelessly, despite never getting the results they want. What a win for them, so happy. I hope there’s a lot more to come.

Peace out.
