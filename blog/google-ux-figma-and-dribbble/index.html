google ux, figma, and dribbble

Aug 9 2021

One of the cool things I did this summer was stepping into design (properly). I’ve always been intrigued by good visuals — I’ve been making art ever since I was a kid. Starting off with crayons to learning watercolours to being formally trained as an artist to creating graphics and digital art at college, I’ve had quite the ride.

Taking the Google UX Design course was a great learning experience. Obviously, MOOCs aren’t the best way to upskill these days, but there’s a lot of things I learnt, some which changed the way I think about products and their design. A few of the very cool things I learnt are wireframing, prototyping, building user personas, mapping user journeys, and how important empathy is in design.

As soon as I finished the course, I couldn’t not start designing right away. Hence began my experiments with Figma. My first project of my own was a simple re-creation of Spotify’s home page.

By now, I’d gotten a good grip on Figma. Like every other design enthusiast, the next thing I started looking at was Dribbble. This platform blew my mind the first time I looked at it. I was very intrigued by the sheer creativity that people put in to make UI that looks great. I wanted to put out some of my work as well. This started my run on Dribbble.

I’m looking forward to exploring this a lot more.
