cold emails, metrics, and root cause analysis

Aug 23 2021

At the start of 2021, I promised myself I’d do a few things for sure this year. Excited to tell y’all that I’ve ticked off a checkbox—sending a cold email. I was hesitant to apply for internships over the last few months due to a fear of a lack of knowledge. I can now tell you in all honesty that, that is time gone down the drain. No regrets because I’ve learnt a lot, nevertheless.

Sent a couple of cold emails this week, it was a really interesting experience. The most enjoyable part for me, so far, was writing a compelling subject line. Another thing I learnt along the process is that sometimes we get too worried about the outcome—what’s most important is to make progress.

Last Saturday, I attended a talk on Product Metrics by Moinak Bandyopadhyay as a part of Insurjo by The Product Folks. Let me tell you that it was some of the most enriching couple of hours I’ve had in a while. Moinak sir is a god with product metrics. I learnt a lot of interesting things: the power of analytics, activation, retention, defining the North Star, and product-market fit, to list a few. I’ve always loved looking at data and capturing insights, so this talk really got me a lot more intrigued. (I remember the first time I saw insights about the music I listened to on Wynk in 2019. Before you ask, yes, I’ve shifted to Spotify. Those insights blew my mind, I never realized companies stored that data and could show it to you in such interesting fashion.) Realizing that analytics is a must-have skill for product management, I’ve started learning how to work with Mixpanel with the help of this course by Product School.

Another nice habit(?) I picked up, as of late, is binging PM interview videos on YouTube. I absolutely love the content Exponent puts up. I’ve been watching a lot of the problem-solving interviews, and I’ve understood how one can find the root cause of a problem. Let me try explaining.

Consider the problem statement: Netflix is seeing a drop in DAU, how would you assess the situation?
Here’s how we can approach this: Root cause can be identified only when we look into all the factors that might have been involved. A systematic way to go about this would be:

  • Start with technical challenges: have we seen this drop post a new update, is there a bug, is there some issue with the metrics we are receiving
  • Next comes user behaviour analysis: is the drop in new or old users, is there a trend among a specific segment of users
  • Third, sentimental analysis: has there been negative PR about Netflix, have we put up content that can be off-putting to users, hence leading to drop, have we had any issues with customer care
  • Fourth, demographic analysis: is this drop happening in a particular location, particular age group
  • Lastly, competitor analysis: has the competition recently put out a new feature that might have pulled our user base, have they made their service cheaper, do they have content that we are lacking on

Considering I’ve sent a few cold emails, I hope these learnings come useful if I can land any interviews.

Peace out.
